## About me ##
<p style='text-align: justify;'> My name is Marie Beigelman. I am a second-year PhD candidate in Economics at the University of Barcelona supervised by UB Professor [Judit Vall Castello](https://www.juditvall.com/).  

I am an applied economist working on Public Economics questions, with a special interest in gender inequality, health, and intra-familiar violence. For the first chapter of my thesis, I have been investigating the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on intimate partner violence in Spain.  I am currently working on the persistence of intra-familiar violence and its effects on the health of descendants. You can find out more about my research [here](https://mariebeigelman.github.io/research) 
 
I was born and grew up in France, where I studied Statistical Engineering in the "Grande Ecole" *ENSAE Paristech*. I also hold a Master in Public Policy and Development from the *Paris School of Economics*. 
Before starting my PhD I worked for two years as a statistician-economist specialized in socioeconomics studies for professional unions in the health sector. </p>
